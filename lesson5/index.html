Community chambers of commerce started in Kumase a region in Ghana and later spread to in the Accra, becoming city chambers of commerce as communities developed and became larger. Community chambers of commerce are smaller and most have a limit on numbers of members.

Spotlight 1

A chamber of commerce may be a voluntary or a mandatory association of business firms belonging to different trades and industries. They serve as spokespeople and representatives of a business community. They differ from country to country.

Spotlight 2

A chamber of commerce is an association or network of businesspeople designed to promote and protect the interests of its members.

Spotlight 3

Business owners in towns and cities form these local societies to advocate on behalf of the business community. Local businesses are members, and they elect a board of directors or executive council to set policy for the chamber.
